Sungka

A Classic Filipino Game of Strategy and Skill

What is Sungka?

Sungka is a traditional Filipino board game played with shells, stones, or seeds. The game uses a carved wooden board with 14 small holes (called *bahay*) and 2 large end holes (called *ulo* or *head*). It’s a test of strategy, timing, and counting skills, commonly played by two players.

How to Play Sungka

  1. The game board has 7 small holes on each side and one large hole (your “home”) at the end.
  2. Each small hole is filled with 7 shells or stones. The big holes start empty.
  3. Players take turns picking up all pieces from one of their holes and distributing them counterclockwise, one per hole.
  4. If your last piece lands in your own home, you get another turn.
  5. If the last piece lands in an empty hole on your side, and the opposite hole has shells, you capture them.
  6. Game ends when one side's small holes are empty. Player with most shells in their home wins.

A Bit of History

Sungka is part of the *mancala* family of games found throughout Southeast Asia, Africa, and the Middle East. In the Philippines, it became a staple among families, especially in the Visayas and Mindanao regions. Boards are often beautifully hand-carved, passed down through generations, and played during relaxed afternoons or family gatherings.
